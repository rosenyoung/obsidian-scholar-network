# Eugene Fama

## Profile

Eugene Fama is the Robert R. McCormick Distinguished Service Professor of Finance at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business. Nobel Laureate in Economics (2013, shared with Hansen and Shiller). With 417,000+ Google Scholar citations, he is the most cited scholar in finance and one of the most cited in all of economics. He is the originator of the efficient market hypothesis (JF 1970, 48,600 cites), co-creator of the Fama-MacBeth regression methodology (JPE 1973, 21,900 cites), and — with Kenneth French — builder of the three-factor (JFE 1993, 40,600 cites) and five-factor (JFE 2015, 12,300 cites) asset pricing models that define the baseline against which virtually all empirical asset pricing research is measured. His corporate governance work with Michael Jensen ("Separation of Ownership and Control," JLE 1983, 58,800 cites) is also foundational. Publication record spans 60+ years across JF, JFE, JPE, AER, RFS, and every major economics and finance journal.

## Notes

- **Gatekeeper justification:** Fama is not merely a gatekeeper — he is the architect of the field. The efficient market hypothesis, the Fama-French factor models, and the Fama-MacBeth methodology are the three pillars on which modern empirical asset pricing is built. 417,000+ citations place him as the most cited finance scholar in history. Nobel Prize 2013.
- **The Fama-French partnership:** The ~40-paper, 30+ year collaboration with [[Kenneth French]] is the most consequential coauthorship in finance. It spans: (1) the original size and value factors (JFE 1993), (2) the five-factor extension (JFE 2015), (3) systematic dissection of anomalies (JF 2008, RFS 2016), (4) international evidence (JFE 2012, 2017), (5) corporate finance (dividends, financing, capital structure), and (6) mutual fund performance. French already has a scholar note in the vault.
- **Three defining contributions:** (1) Efficient Market Hypothesis — "Efficient Capital Markets" (JF 1970, 48,600 cites) defined three forms of market efficiency and shaped the field for decades. (2) Factor models — the three-factor (1993) and five-factor (2015) models are the universal benchmark for risk adjustment. (3) Fama-MacBeth regression — the two-pass methodology (JPE 1973, 21,900 cites) is the standard approach for cross-sectional asset pricing tests.
- **Corporate governance pillar:** Often overlooked in asset pricing profiles, but the Fama-Jensen papers on agency problems and organizational forms (JLE 1983, 58,800 cites; JFE 1985) are among the most cited papers in corporate finance and law & economics.
