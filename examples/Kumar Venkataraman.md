# Kumar Venkataraman

## Profile

Professor of Finance at Southern Methodist University (Cox School of Business). One of the leading empiricists in fixed-income market microstructure, with a two-decade body of work on trading costs, transparency, and market design in corporate bond, structured credit, and equity markets. Published 3 papers in the Journal of Finance (including a solo-authored JF in 2001), 8 in the Journal of Financial Economics, 3 in the Review of Financial Studies, and 3 in the JFQA. Co-authored the authoritative JFQA survey of fixed-income microstructure (2020) with Bessembinder and Spatt. His work on the TRACE reporting system's impact on corporate bond trading costs is widely cited by regulators and practitioners.

## Notes

Core member of the Bessembinder–Venkataraman research cluster that dominates fixed-income microstructure. The Bessembinder partnership (9 joint papers) is one of the most prolific in this subfield — they co-authored the definitive JFQA survey, the TRACE transparency studies, and the corporate bond liquidity papers. A second strong cluster with Amber Anand (6 papers) focuses on institutional order handling, trading desk performance, and market fragility — bridging equity and bond microstructure.

Notable for having a solo-authored JF paper early in career (2001) — unusual in microstructure where coauthorship is the norm. Recent trajectory shows expansion into AI/machine learning applications in bond pricing (CP+ paper), suggesting a move toward the intersection of `market design` and `machine learning`.

The Venkataraman–Bessembinder pair is the natural complement to the Menkveld-centered European microstructure group. Both study market design but Venkataraman focuses on OTC/dealer markets (bonds) while Menkveld focuses on electronic order-driven markets (equities/HFT).
