# Stacey Jacobsen

## Profile

Professor of Finance at Southern Methodist University (Cox School of Business). Unusual for genuinely bridging two distinct areas: fixed-income market microstructure (with Venkataraman and Bessembinder) and corporate finance / M&A (with Ben-David and Bhattacharya). Published 3 papers in the Journal of Finance, 2 in the Journal of Financial Economics, plus Review of Finance, Management Science, and Foundations & Trends in Finance. Her most-cited paper (511 citations) on liquidity measurement with Holden solved a key practical problem in microstructure research. Total Google Scholar citations: 1,662.

## Notes

A genuine cross-topic bridge — one of the few scholars who publishes at the top level in both market microstructure and corporate finance. Her coauthor network reflects this split cleanly: the Venkataraman/Bessembinder/Maxwell/Holden cluster maps to `liquidity`, while the Ben-David/Bhattacharya cluster maps to `mergers acquisitions`.

Her Holden coauthorship (4 papers) is methodological — developing better ways to measure liquidity in fast markets. The Holden-Jacobsen (2014, JF) liquidity measurement paper is a "tools" paper used by hundreds of subsequent empirical studies. This is a different kind of contribution from the substantive bond microstructure work with Venkataraman.

At the same institution as Venkataraman (both SMU Cox), which explains the deep collaboration. The SMU Cox microstructure group (Venkataraman + Jacobsen) is a notable hub, especially for corporate bond market research.

Solo-authored JFE paper (Death of the Deal, 2014) on withdrawn M&A deals and CEO quality — demonstrates independent capability outside the coauthorship clusters. The recent Ben-David et al. (JF 2025) paper challenges the assumption that acquisition announcement returns predict actual value creation — a provocative finding for the M&A literature.
